# About Me

## Who I Am
I'm Namare Shakib Angkon, a dedicated Computer Science and Engineering student at United International University. My expertise bridges the gap between software and hardware. I specialize in building AI-driven applications (leveraging LLMs, NLP, and real-time object detection), crafting accessible full-stack web platforms, and engineering embedded systems like portable hardware solutions.

## What Drives Me
My work spans a diverse set of technical domains:

**Machine Learning & AI**: I'm deeply passionate about applying Artificial Intelligence to solve real-world problems. Whether it is building ARGUS, an on-device AI navigation system for the visually impaired, or implementing intelligent risk-flag generation for healthcare platforms, I focus on systems that make a tangible impact.

**Embedded Systems & Hardware**: I don't just write software; I enjoy building the hardware that runs it. Projects like Portable Braille—a low-cost printer that embosses digital text onto A4 paper using Raspberry Pi, stepper motors, and 3D printing—highlight my ability to combine physical engineering with code.

**Full-Stack Engineering**: I enjoy crafting complete, accessible platforms. Through projects like ShareStrength and Maternify, I've designed and developed web applications that empower users, particularly those with disabilities or unique healthcare needs. 

## My Approach
I believe in:
- **Accessibility & Impact**: Technology is most powerful when it empowers those who need it most.
- **Hardware-Software Synergy**: The best solutions often require understanding both the code and the physical devices it runs on.
- **Continuous Learning**: From winning hackathons to securing Pre-Seed funding, I'm always looking for the next challenge to learn and grow.
